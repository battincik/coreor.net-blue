# Designing for Privacy: Principles and Practices

Privacy-by-design is about embedding privacy considerations throughout the
product lifecycle — from discovery to deprecation. This article gives concrete
examples and guidance for teams building data-driven features.

## Minimise data collection

Collect only what you need. Prefer derived or aggregated signals over raw PII.

## Consent and transparency

Make consent flows clear and scoped. Provide easy-to-find privacy settings and
explain what the data is used for in plain language.

## Data retention and access controls

Define retention windows and automate deletion. Use RBAC and least privilege
for access to production data.

## Practical steps for engineers

- Pseudonymize identifiers where possible
- Log less: avoid storing raw PII in logs
- Encrypt sensitive data at rest and in transit

## Compliance and audits

Work with legal to map product features to compliance requirements (e.g.,
GDPR). Maintain an audit trail of data access and processing decisions.
